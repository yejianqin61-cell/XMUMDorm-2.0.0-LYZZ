# Dorm: Building a Digital Community Infrastructure for XMUM

## Overview

Dorm is a student-developed digital community platform designed specifically for the students of Xiamen University Malaysia.

The project was initiated with a simple observation:

Although students spend years studying and living together on campus, communication, information sharing, club activities, second-hand trading, and community interaction remain scattered across multiple disconnected platforms.

As a result, students often struggle to:

- Discover campus events and opportunities
- Connect with peers who share similar interests
- Access reliable campus information
- Participate in student communities
- Exchange goods and services within the campus ecosystem

Dorm aims to address these challenges by creating a unified digital space for the XMUM community.

------

## The Vision

Our vision is to build the digital community infrastructure for XMUM.

Just as physical facilities such as libraries, lecture halls, and student centers support campus life offline, Dorm seeks to provide the digital infrastructure that supports campus life online.

We believe that every modern university deserves a dedicated platform where students can communicate, collaborate, share resources, and build meaningful connections within their own community.

## What Dorm Provides

Dorm integrates multiple aspects of student life into a single platform:

### Community Interaction

A campus discussion space where students can share experiences, exchange ideas, and participate in conversations relevant to university life.

### Student Marketplace

A trusted environment for students to buy, sell, and exchange second-hand items within the university community.

### Club and Society Hub

A centralized space where student organizations can promote activities, recruit members, and engage with the wider student population.

### Campus Information Exchange

A channel that helps students discover opportunities, events, announcements, and useful information more efficiently.

### Student Networking

Tools that encourage students from different faculties, years, and backgrounds to connect and interact beyond traditional social circles.
